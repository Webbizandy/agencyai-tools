## What Is ServiceTitan?

ServiceTitan is the dominant CRM and business management platform for field service contractors ‚Äî specifically HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and similar trades.

Think of it as Salesforce + QuickBooks + a dispatch board, all built specifically for guys with trucks and toolboxes.

**Core functions:**
- **Scheduling & Dispatch:** Real-time job board, drag-and-drop scheduling, GPS tracking
- **Mobile App for Techs:** Digital job history, price book, invoicing from the truck
- **Customer Management:** Full CRM with call tracking, booking history, membership programs
- **Invoicing & Payments:** Generate invoices on-site, accept payments via mobile
- **Marketing Automation:** Email/SMS campaigns, review requests, customer retention tools
- **Reporting & Analytics:** Revenue tracking, tech performance, conversion rates

**Who uses it?**
- HVAC companies (90% of large HVAC shops use ServiceTitan)
- Plumbing contractors
- Electrical contractors
- Garage door, pest control, appliance repair, pool service

**Who it's NOT for:**
- General contractors (try Buildertrend or CoConstruct)
- Small handyman businesses under $200K/year (too expensive, try Jobber)
- Non-field service businesses

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I get ServiceTitan pricing without a sales call?

No. ServiceTitan requires a demo and quote. Expect a 30-45 minute sales call where they'll ask about your revenue, number of trucks, current tools, etc.

**Pro tip:** Be honest about your revenue. They won't sell to you if you're too small (under $300K/year). If you're below that, ask them to recommend Jobber or Housecall Pro ‚Äî they'll respect the honesty.

### Does ServiceTitan have a free trial?

No free trial. They offer a **demo account** where you can click around, but you can't use it for real jobs.

### Is there a setup fee?

Yes. Onboarding costs $1,500-$8,000 depending on:
- How much data you're migrating (old CRM, QuickBooks, spreadsheets)
- How many users need training
- Whether you want custom integrations

### Can I use ServiceTitan if I'm a solo contractor?

Technically yes, but it's **massive overkill**. If you're a one-person show, use Jobber ($49/mo) or Housecall Pro ($49/mo) instead. ServiceTitan is built for teams.

### Does ServiceTitan integrate with QuickBooks?

Yes. Two-way sync with QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop. Invoices, payments, and customer data flow automatically.

### Can I use ServiceTitan if I'm not HVAC/plumbing/electrical?

Officially, ServiceTitan is built for "residential field service contractors." In practice:

## Limitations & Drawbacks (The Honest Take)

### 1. **It's Expensive**

$500-$2,000/mo is a real budget commitment. For contractors under $500K/year, that's a big chunk of profit.

**Reality check:** If you're doing $300K/year and netting 10% profit ($30K), spending $6K-$10K/year on software is tough. You'll see ROI, but the upfront investment hurts.

**Alternative:** Start with Jobber ($49/mo), grow to $500K, *then* upgrade to ServiceTitan.

---

### 2. **Onboarding Is a Slog**

ServiceTitan is powerful, but setup takes 30-60 days and 20-40 hours of work. You need to:
- Migrate all your customer data
- Build your entire price book (every service, every part)
- Set up your call tracking numbers
- Train your entire team (office + field techs)

**What agencies need to know:** If you're selling ServiceTitan integrations, budget 10-15 hours for the API setup. It's not plug-and-play.

---

### 3. **Overkill for Small Shops**

If you're a solo plumber or a 2-person HVAC shop, ServiceTitan is like buying a semi-truck to deliver pizzas. It works, but it's ridiculous.

**Use case mismatch:**
- Solo contractor doing $200K/year ‚Üí Use Jobber ($49/mo)
- 2-3 person team doing $300K-$500K ‚Üí Use Housecall Pro ($169/mo)
- 5+ person team doing $500K+ ‚Üí Now ServiceTitan makes sense

---

### 4. **No Month-to-Month Plans**

ServiceTitan requires annual contracts. You're locked in for 12 months minimum.

**Cancellation policy:** If you cancel mid-contract, you owe the remaining months. No prorated refunds.

---

### 5. **Sales Process Is Intense**

Getting pricing requires:
1. Fill out a form
2. Schedule a demo call (30-45 min)
3. Get a custom quote
4. Sign an annual contract

Some contractors find this annoying. If you just want to "try it for $99/mo," you'll be disappointed.

**Why they do this:** ServiceTitan's customers are $1M+ revenue businesses. They need implementation support, training, and account management. A self-serve free trial doesn't make sense at that level.
