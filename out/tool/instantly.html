Andy's Honest Take

Instantly solved the one problem that kills cold email: landing in spam.

Most cold email tools burn your domain reputation in a week. You send 500 emails, 400 go to spam, Gmail flags you, and suddenly your main business email is getting filtered. Instantly prevents this with email warmup and account rotation.

Here's how it works: You buy multiple domains (youragency1.com, youragency2.com, etc.), set up email accounts on each, connect them to Instantly, and it warms them up by sending fake emails between accounts to build sender reputation. Then when you launch real campaigns, it rotates sends across all accounts so no single email looks spammy.

The result? I've run campaigns with 70-80% open rates. That's insane for cold email. Most tools give you 15-25% if you're lucky.

The unlimited email accounts feature is what makes Instantly different. Lemlist charges per seat ($59+ per email account). Smartlead has limits. Instantly Growth plan ($97/mo) and up? Connect as many email accounts as you want. I know agencies running 30-50 accounts on one Instantly subscription.

But nobody talks about the hidden costs:

You need to buy domains ($10-15 each). You need email hosting (Google Workspace at $6/user/month or SMTP service at $15-30/month for multiple accounts). For 10 email accounts, you're paying $150-250/month in total costs (Instantly subscription + domains + email hosting).

The warmup takes 2-4 weeks before you can send real campaigns. You can't spin up new domains and blast 500 emails the next day. Each account needs to gradually build sender reputation. This means planning ahead.

The interface has improved but still has a learning curve. It's not Mailchimp-level intuitive. Expect to spend 3-4 hours watching tutorials and testing before you're comfortable.

Where Instantly actually crushes it:

The campaign builder is powerful. A/B testing, dynamic personalization ({{firstName}}, {{company}}, custom fields), conditional logic, follow-up sequences. You can build sophisticated multi-step campaigns.

Reply detection works perfectly. When someone replies, Instantly automatically pauses that contact so you don't keep emailing them. Sounds basic, but some tools mess this up and you end up looking stupid.

The analytics are detailed. Open rates, click rates, reply rates broken down by campaign, email variant, time of day, day of week. You can actually optimize based on data instead of guessing.

The deliverability monitoring shows you which domains are getting flagged, which email accounts have reputation issues, which campaigns are performing poorly. This is gold for maintaining healthy sending.

For agencies doing outbound: The unlimited accounts feature is game-changing. You can run campaigns for 5-10 clients from one dashboard, keep their accounts completely separate, track performance individually, and still pay just one subscription.

One warning: Some industries get flagged more than others. If you're in crypto, forex, CBD, online casino, or 'make money online' niches, expect deliverability issues no matter which tool you use. Instantly can't save you from Gmail's spam filters if you're in a flagged industry.

Pricing reality:

Growth ($97/mo): Unlimited email accounts, 5,000 emails/month, all core features. This is the sweet spot for most agencies.

Hypergrowth ($197/mo): Unlimited emails, priority support, advanced analytics. Only worth it if you're sending 10K+ emails/month.

Light Speed ($358/mo): White-label, agency features, dedicated support. Only for agencies managing multiple clients with 20K+ emails/month.

The setup process (what they don't tell you):

1. Buy 3-10 domains from Namecheap or similar ($10-15 each) 2. Set up Google Workspace or SMTP service for email hosting ($6-30/account) 3. Connect domains to Instantly 4. Wait 2-4 weeks for warmup (sending fake emails to build reputation) 5. Build your first campaign 6. Test with 50-100 emails 7. Optimize based on results 8. Scale to full campaign

Total time from signup to first real campaign: 3-4 weeks minimum. Factor this into your planning.

The copywriting still matters more than the tool. I see people blame Instantly when they get 2% reply rates. The tool is fine - their cold email copy sucks. You can have perfect deliverability and still get ignored if your emails are generic garbage.
