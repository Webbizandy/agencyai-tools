Andy's Take

The free CRM is actually good - not a bait-and-switch. But once you need automation, email sequences, or reporting, they hit you with $800+/month pricing. Great for startups, expensive for scale.

What It Does

**What it does:** Customer relationship management platform that tracks every interaction with prospects and clients — emails, calls, meetings, deals, everything.

**Why it matters:** You can't scale if you're managing clients in spreadsheets or your head. HubSpot gives you a real system — see where every deal is, what stage they're in, and what needs to happen next.

**Who it's for:** Agencies managing multiple clients, consultants juggling prospects and active clients, or anyone who's tired of losing track of follow-ups.

**Andy's notes:** The free tier is insanely good. I've seen agencies run their entire sales operation on it without paying a cent. Once you need marketing automation or advanced reporting, the paid tiers get pricey fast. But for pure CRM? Hard to beat free.

Andy's Honest Take

HubSpot's free CRM is the best free CRM. Not 'good for free' - actually good. This is rare in software.

Unlimited contacts, deal tracking, company records, email integration, meeting scheduling, live chat, basic reporting - all free forever. No credit card required. No sneaky limits that force you to upgrade. It's legitimately useful.

I know agencies running their entire business on the free tier for 2-3 years before upgrading. If you're just starting, need a real CRM, and have zero budget, start here.

But here's where HubSpot gets you:

The moment you need automation (email sequences, lead scoring, workflow triggers), you're looking at Marketing Hub Professional ($800+/month). The free tier doesn't include any marketing automation.

Want to send bulk emails? Need Advanced reporting? Custom dashboards? Sales sequences? You're paying for Professional plans ($450-1,800/month depending on which hubs you need).

HubSpot is built like an a-la-carte restaurant. CRM is free. Want automation? That's Marketing Hub. Want sales sequences? That's Sales Hub. Want custom reporting? That's Enterprise. Need all three? Now you're at $2,000-3,000/month.

The pricing model is: Free CRM to get you hooked, then charge for features you actually need to run a business. It works because the CRM is good enough that switching to another tool after 12 months feels painful.

Where HubSpot actually shines:

The interface is clean and intuitive. Non-technical team members can learn it in a day. The deal pipeline view is visual and easy to understand. Task management is built in and actually usable.

The contact records are comprehensive. Every email, every meeting, every website visit, every form submission - all logged automatically. You can see a complete history of every interaction with a lead.

The email integration is seamless. Connect Gmail or Outlook, and every email you send gets logged in the CRM automatically. You don't have to manually track conversations.

The reporting on paid plans is excellent. Custom dashboards, attribution tracking, ROI reporting, funnel analytics. If you're managing multiple campaigns or team members, this is gold.

The ecosystem is massive. Thousands of integrations, tons of templates, huge community, endless tutorials. If you have a problem, someone has solved it and written about it.

For agencies selling to SMBs: HubSpot is overkill and too expensive. Your clients don't need enterprise-level marketing automation. They need simple CRM + email + follow-up. Use GoHighLevel or Pipedrive instead.

For agencies selling to mid-market or enterprise: HubSpot makes sense. These clients have budgets, need robust reporting, want integrations with their tech stack. The $800-2,000/month price tag doesn't scare them.

For agencies using it internally: Start with free CRM. Upgrade to paid plans only when you actually need specific features (automation, sequences, advanced reporting). Don't upgrade just because HubSpot salespeople pressure you.

The sales team is aggressive. They'll call you 47 times trying to upsell. Be ready to say no until you actually need paid features. The free CRM is good enough for most agencies under $20K/month revenue.

Customer support on free tier is basically nonexistent. You get access to community forums and documentation. Paid plans get actual support, but even then it's hit or miss. Expect 24-48 hours for responses unless you're Enterprise.

Pricing breakdown:

Free CRM: $0, unlimited contacts, basic features, perfect for startups

Marketing Hub Professional: $800/mo (billed annually), marketing automation, email sequences, workflows, advanced reporting

Sales Hub Professional: $450/mo, sales sequences, calling, advanced deal management

Service Hub Professional: $450/mo, ticketing, customer service tools

Bundle all three: $2,000+/month for Professional tier

The math: If you need more than one Hub, compare GoHighLevel ($297/mo for everything) vs HubSpot ($2,000+/mo). For most agencies, GHL makes more sense.
